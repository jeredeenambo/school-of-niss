    • Exploring Opportunities at NYS Institute of Social Studies

    Read more about our the course

    At NYS Institute of Social Studies, a world of opportunities awaits those with a passion for making a positive impact on society. With a range of courses designed to cater to different interests and career aspirations, students can embark on a fulfilling journey of personal growth and professional development. Let's delve into the diverse array of programs offered by the institute, each paving the way for meaningful contributions in various fields

    CHILD CARE

    The Child Care course equips students with the skills and knowledge necessary to provide nurturing and supportive environments for children's growth and development. Graduates of this program may find employment opportunities in daycare centers, preschools, or as nannies or childcare providers in private households, This course is being offered currently at level 6 (diploma)

    SOCIAL WORK AND COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT

    With a focus on community empowerment and social justice, the Social Work and Community Development course prepares individuals to address societal challenges and advocate for marginalized communities. Graduates may pursue careers in social service agencies, non-profit organizations, or government agencies working in community development. we offer this from level 5 to 6

    COUNSELING AND PSYCHOLOGY

    Unlock the mysteries of the human mind and emotions through our counseling and psychology programs. The Counseling and Psychology program equips students with the expertise to offer emotional support, guidance, and therapy to individuals facing personal or psychological challenges. Graduates may find employment in counseling centers, mental health clinics, schools, or private practice settings

    LIBRARY AND INFORMATION SCIENCE

    Dive into the world of knowledge organization and dissemination with our library and information science courses. Master the principles of cataloging, classification, and information retrieval in the digital age. Graduates can find employment in libraries, archives, museums, or information centers, serving as guardians of knowledge and culture

    ECDE (Early Childhood Development Education):

    With a focus on early childhood education and development, the ECDE program trains educators to work with young children in preschools, kindergartens, or early intervention programs. Graduates may pursue careers as early childhood educators, curriculum developers, or childcare. This course is being offered at Diploma level only (DECTE) and the minimum entry grade is C plain
